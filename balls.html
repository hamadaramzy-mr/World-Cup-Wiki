Official Match Balls Evolution

From the leather balls of the 1970s to the 2026 Trionda. Filter by era, and like your favorite.

1970

Telstar

The iconic black and white leather ball introduced for the television era.

1982

Tango España

The last all-leather World Cup ball, with a water-resistant coating for rainy conditions.

1986

Azteca

The first fully synthetic World Cup ball, designed to hold up in Mexico's varied climate.

1994

Questra

Used a syntactic foam layer to improve speed and control on the pitch.

2002

Fevernova

A radical, colorful redesign for the first World Cup held in Asia.

2006

Teamgeist

Reduced panel count improved roundness and consistency in flight.

2010

Jabulani

Known for its speed and unpredictable, curving flight path.

2014

Brazuca

Six-panel design praised by players for its stability and grip.

2018

Telstar 18

A modern nod to the original 1970 Telstar, with an embedded NFC chip.

2022

Al Rihla

The fastest World Cup ball ever tested, built with connected ball technology for VAR.

2026

Trionda Current

The official ball of the 2026 tournament, its name meaning "three waves" for the three co-host nations.
